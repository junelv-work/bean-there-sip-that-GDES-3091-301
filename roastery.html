The Alchemy of Heat & Time

Coffee roasting uses high temperatures to develop the flavours and aromas locked inside green coffee beans. Through careful heat application, chemical reactions create the spectrum of roast levels from light to dark, each with distinct flavour profiles.

Roast Levels

From light and acidic to dark and bold

Light Roast

205°C

Known as New England Roast, this stage occurs mid first crack when coffee develops its true aroma and becomes drinkable. Lightly roasted to preserve the bean's original characteristics, this level is ideal for cupping, allowing the coffee's origin flavours to shine through without roast interference.

  • Flavour: Bright, acidic, fruity
  • Caffeine: Highest content
  • Body: Light, tea-like
  • First Crack: Just completed
Cinnamon Roast New England Roast Half City

Medium Roast

210°C - 221°C

Full Medium Roast follows first crack completion, featuring minimal oil and balanced origin-to-roast flavours. This popular level offers ideal harmony between acidity and body, making it our recommended choice for most coffees.

  • Flavour: Balanced, sweet, nutty
  • Caffeine: Medium content
  • Body: Medium, rounded
  • Development: Between cracks
American Roast City Roast Breakfast Roast

Dark Roast

221°C - 227°C

Dark Roast reaches mid second crack with oily beans and pronounced smoke. Roast flavours dominate, reducing acidity for a bold, smoky profile—the darkest we typically recommend while preserving some coffee integrity.

  • Flavour: Bold, smoky, bitter
  • Caffeine: Lowest content
  • Body: Heavy, syrupy
  • Second Crack: Fully developed

Key Components Explained

Burner & Heating System

Provides precise heat control, typically using gas or electric elements reaching 240°C+

Rotating Drum

Rotates beans for even roasting, typically at 40-60 RPM, with perforations for airflow

Airflow System

Controls hot air circulation and removes chaff (silver skin) during roasting

Probes & Sensors

Monitor bean temperature, drum temperature, and roast time for precision

Cooling Tray

Rapidly cools beans after roasting to stop the cooking process immediately

Types of Coffee Roasters

Drum Roaster

Traditional method using a rotating drum for even, consistent roasts

Fluid Bed Roaster

Uses hot air to suspend and roast beans (like a popcorn popper)

Commercial Roaster

Large-scale roasters handling 1kg to 60kg batches for wholesale

Home Roaster

Small batch roasters for enthusiasts (100g-1kg capacity)

Brewing Methods

Different techniques, unique experiences

4-5 min

French Press

Easy Immersion

A full-bodied, rich brew where coffee grounds steep directly in hot water. The metal filter allows oils and fine particles to pass through, creating a robust flavour profile.

Water Temp: 200°F
Steep Time: 4 min
Grind: Coarse
2-3 min

Pour Over

Moderate Drip

Hot water is poured evenly over coffee grounds in a filter. This method allows for precise control over brewing variables, resulting in a clean, bright, and nuanced cup.

Water Temp: 205°F
Brew Time: 3 min
Grind: Medium
25-30 sec

Espresso

Advanced Pressure

Highly pressurized hot water is forced through finely-ground coffee, producing a concentrated shot with a rich crema. The foundation for lattes, cappuccinos, and more.

Water Temp: 200°F
Pressure: 9 bar
Grind: Fine
12-24 hrs

Cold Brew

Very Easy Cold Immersion

Coffee grounds steep in cold water for 12-24 hours, producing a smooth, low-acidity concentrate. Perfect for iced coffee and refreshing summer drinks.

Water Temp: Cold
Steep Time: 18 hrs
Grind: Coarse
1-2 min

AeroPress

Easy-Moderate Pressure + Immersion

A versatile brewing device that uses air pressure to push hot water through coffee grounds. Creates a clean, smooth cup similar to espresso but with less bitterness.

Water Temp: 175-205°F
Total Time: 1.5 min
Grind: Fine-Medium
5-10 min

Moka Pot

Easy Stovetop Pressure

A stovetop brewer that uses steam pressure to push hot water through coffee grounds. Produces a strong, concentrated coffee similar to espresso but with more body.

Heat Source: Stove
Brew Time: 7 min
Grind: Medium-Fine

Toronto's vibrant coffee scene

The Third Place

Cafes serve as "third places". Not home, not work, but community hubs where people connect, create, and find inspiration. Toronto's diverse neighborhoods each offer unique cafe experiences that reflect their local character.

From cozy study spots in the Annex to trendy roasteries in the West End, each cafe contributes to the city's rich tapestry of coffee culture.

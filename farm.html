Processing Methods

How coffee cherries transform into green beans

Natural/Dry Process

Coffee cherries are dried in the sun with the fruit still attached, creating fruity, complex flavours.

  • Sun-dried for 3-6 weeks
  • Berry & wine-like notes
  • Common in Ethiopia, Yemen

Washed/Wet Process

Fruit is removed before drying, resulting in cleaner, brighter, and more acidic profiles.

  • Fermentation & washing
  • Clean & bright acidity
  • Preferred in high altitudes

Honey Process

A hybrid method where some fruit mucilage remains during drying, offering balanced sweetness.

  • Mucilage retained (20-100%)
  • Sweet & syrupy body
  • Popular in Costa Rica

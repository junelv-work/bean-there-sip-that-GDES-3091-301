Crisis & Discovery

The environmental challenges facing coffee production

Climate Change Threat

Rising temperatures and unpredictable weather patterns are reducing suitable land for coffee cultivation in some regions. Arabica coffee is particularly vulnerable, requiring specific temperature ranges to thrive.

60% of wild coffee species face extinction

Water Usage

Coffee production is water-intensive. From irrigation to processing, it takes approximately 140 liters of water to produce one cup of coffee. Sustainable water management practices are becoming essential.

140L water per cup of coffee

Deforestation

Traditional sun-grown coffee often replaces forest ecosystems, reducing biodiversity and increasing soil erosion. Shade-grown coffee preserves tree canopy and provides habitat for wildlife.

2.5M hectares of forest lost for coffee farms

Towards Sustainable Coffee

How we can make a difference

Choose Certified

Look for certifications like Fair Trade, Rainforest Alliance, Organic, and Bird Friendly.

Support Local Roasters

Local roasting reduces transportation emissions and supports community businesses.

Reduce Waste

Use reusable cups, avoid single-serving pods, and compost coffee grounds.

Direct Trade

Support roasters who buy directly from farmers, ensuring fair prices and transparency.

Every Cup Makes a Difference

Your coffee choices impact farmers, ecosystems, and climate. Choose consciously.
